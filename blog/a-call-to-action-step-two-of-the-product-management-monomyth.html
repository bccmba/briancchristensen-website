A Call to Action - Step Two of the Product Management Monomyth

In Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring, Gandalf the Grey is riding into the Shire where he is meet by Frodo Baggins who rides with him for a little bit. In the conversation, Frodo reminds Gandalf that before he showed up the Baggins' were a very respected family. After Bilbo's adventures, however, Gandalf is now labeled a public enemy. To which Gandalf replies he never took Bilbo on any adventure he merely gave him a nudge in the right direction.

So this goes for many of the heros who are called to action. As they in the state of the status quo our usually unlikely hero is given a call to action. Many times this is given by someone else, as in the case with both Bilbo and Frodo Baggins who both were nudged by Gandalf. Although at other times a specific event will cause the hero to move from the status quo and be called to action.

This is the same case with Product Managers. There are going to be times when you are going to be called to action and in some cases there will be a person calling you to action. In others there will be a specific event calling you in action. In either case the trick is to answer the call.

Call to Action via a person

The most typical call to action a Product Manager is going to get usually comes from a person. Most of the time this is in the form of a stakeholder but it could be from other members of your product team or even the development team themselves.

Much like the nudge Gandalf gave to both Biblo and Frodo, the call to action from the stakeholders should be a nudge as well. This is where the real trick lies with this call to action. Many people, stakeholders included, not only like to come up with great ideas for what your product could do, but they often try to come up with the solution as well. Using scrum terminology, not only do they provide you the what and the why, but they want to supply the how for you as well. As a Product Manager there is where you need to step in and help sort this out. True Gandalf could have just told Frodo, step by step on how to accomplish the journey, but he held back and just gave him the what and the why and let him figure out how to get it done.

As you work with you stakeholders, be sure to help them make this distinction. Work with them on just pairing down their request to the what and the why of their ask. Let them know the team will work on the how, but like how Gandalf joined the Fellowship of the Ring, you are going to including the stakeholder on how their call to action is going to get done.

Sometimes, the call to action from you stakeholders is going to be a large ask, this is where working with them on drilling into the what and the why is going to help. As you can stay focused with them at this level, you as the Product Manager should be able to help them create an MVP of the feature. By doing this, you should be able to create even more checkpoints in the system with places you and the stakeholder can stop and make sure you are moving in the right direction for your product.

Call to action by an event

The other way a Product Manager could get called to make a change in their product would be through an event in the system. As you are working on features with your stakeholders, it is always good to be looking for places you can insert monitoring and reporting functions within your product. This is a great way to have the system start to alert you if it is noticing problems and issues. This can help you hopefully stay ahead of any big issues.

There have been many times in my career where had we placed some simple monitoring within the product we could have caught an issue early. Instead of catching something before it became a problem, we had to scramble, pull all hands on deck and work many late hours for an issue that could have been prevented by putting a simple alert in the code. Whenever and if at all possible I strongly urge you work these into your design of how you are going to solve the what and why. Save yourself for the extra work down the road.

As a Product Manager, all of us are going to get a call to action with our product. It is up to us on how we are going to manage this call. My hope is we can just take the nudge we need and move down the path onto the next step in the Product Manager Monomyth.
