Supernatural Aid - Step Three of the Product Management Monomyth

In the most resent Pixar movie, Soul, 22 is a rebelous soul doing all she can to not go to Earth. There have been several people who have tried to help, but yet 22 wants to stay in the "Great Before." This is until she meets Joe Gardner. Joe has just had his one big break and wants nothing more to get back to Earth. In a strange set of circumstances 22 ends up going into Joe's body, while he ends up in the body of a cat. However, it is acting as a cat, that Joe is able to help 22 feel what it is like to be human, and helps her change her mind and want to go live on Earth.

As a Product Manager, once you have received your Call to Action, now it is time to move into the next step of the Product Manager Monomyth but in order to do this, you will need some Supernatural Aid. I can think of no better group of people to represent a Product Manager's Supernatural Aid than looking toward the Agile Team. I know there are different definitions of who makes up the Agile Team, but for this article, I am going to break it down into the Development Team, the Quality Engineers and the Scrum Masters. The more you can understand all of the teams' jobs, the easier your journey is going to be.

Development Team

The Development team has to be my most favorite member of the Agile Team. This is the part of the team responsible for coming up with the "How" you are going to achieve your Call to Action. In my experience, these are truly the Supernatural Aid you are going to need as a Product Manager to help you with your new feature development for your product. I have always felt like my success has always been determined more so by how great a development team I have rather than on how great a Product Owner I am.

I have always been amazed at watching a developer code. How they can stare at all of those lines and make sense of what everything means is truly amazing. This I think is where you can gain the best understanding of their job. Now I am not advocating all Product Managers go out and learn how to code as well as a developer, but if you can take some time and learn a little it will only add to your skillset. For myself, I worked for a short time as a DBDev and got fairly good at writing queries. This has helped me on more than one occasion as we have been working on a new feature.

Quality Engineer

At my current company, we refer to the Agile Team members who work on quality as Quality Engineers. At other places I have worked we have called them Quality Analysts. Regardless if you are talking about QE or QA, at the end of the day these are the team members who are responsible for the quality of the code going out.

According to Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe), quality is one of the essential items needed in product developments. This built-in quality needs to be there from the beginning. It cannot be added in later, it is what is inherently there. In my experience, a good QE needs to be someone who knows your product as wells as, if not better than the Product Manager. This will help them be able to quickly identify the items they need to check when they are working with the development team. Sometimes these items might not even be in the exact code the development team is updating. However, because of how well they know the product, they know all of the areas the update will affect and can check them before they become a problem for the customer.

I like to spend a lot of time with my QE and help them truly understand the "what" and the "why" of the feature we are developing. Many times I will often ask them to consider me their stakeholder and before we let any code go out, they give me a demo of what they have tested. This gives us a chance to go through everything and to discuss other areas this new code might be touching and test it as well.

Scrum Master

The last person in my Agile Team is the scrum master. My favorite definition of a scrum master comes from SAFe where it says:

The Scrum Master is a servant leader and coach for the team. This role instills the agreed-to Agile process, helps facilitates the removal of impediments to progress, and fosters an environment for high performance, continuous flow, and relentless improvement.

https://www.scaledagileframework.com/agile-teams/

In different companies I have worked for the roll of scrum master has been handled differently. Currently, my company has specificly hired people to be scrum masters. At other companies, the team has just voted on a person to be the scrum master and in addition to their other duties, they have been the scrum master as well. In either case, my involvement as a Product Manager with the scrum master has been large. Mostly because they are the ones who have the responsibility to keep the work in the sprint moving forward. The more in tune you can stay with your scrum master, the easier it is for you as the Product Manager to know where your team is and what you need to have ready for them in the next few sprints.

Last Thought

One last thing in helping you the Product Manager working with this Supernatural Aid you have been given. It is important to learn how to speak the language of all three of their groups. A mistake I see some Product Managers make is thinking they can use the same words, tones and even phrases amoungst all three groups. It has been my experince in the years I have been doing this that this will not work. It is important for you to learn how best to speak with each group because the better you can get at this the easier it will be to help all of the understand more of the "what" and the "why." The clearer you can make this for them, the more amazing the "how" is going to be you
