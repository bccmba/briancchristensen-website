Importance of Feedback

At the end of every sprint, the team will sit down with the group of stakeholders to show them everything they have produced in the last sprint.

As a Product Owner, I have always loved this meeting. It is where I get a chance for the stakeholders to see how amazing my teams are as well as show off what they have produced for them. This last week I got to see not only how important these meetings are for the stakeholders but just how important they can be for my teams.

In the first demo, the stakeholders were very interactive with the teams, asking many questions back to the developers about their stories. Although most of the exchange was praises to the developers, there were a couple of times where they were challenged why they went a particular direction or if they might have been an easier way to accomplish the same tasks. At the end everyone walked away knowing what the team had worked on had met the requirements laid before them as well as having some clear directions in what we needed to do next for the product.

The second demo was amazing too. This team is taking on a huge project of re-writing a 20 year old application and they have been doing some amazing work and this demo was not exception. The only difference was this time none of the stakeholders made any comments on anything any of the team showed them. Not one comment.

The biggest thing happened in the next few days after the demo. The first team, spent the next three days commented all of the time about all of the interactions and comments they had gotten from the stakeholders. Even the team member who had received some of the harshest comments continued to talk about it for three days say how much he loved the demo and working with the stakeholders. In contrast, I spent those same three days doing damage control with my second team. In their mind, because no one had commented on their work they created a narrative in their mind thinking the stakeholders did not like what they were doing and were not happy with their work.

The only difference between the two scenarios was feedback from the stakeholders. Ultimately all of us want to get feedback on the job we are doing and if we are not receiving any, it is easy for us to create negative narratives in our mind, even if that may not be the case.

When you are presented with the opportunity to give feedback, please take advantage of this, you never know how powerful an impact it will have on the other person.
