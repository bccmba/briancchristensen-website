How Product Managers Remove Distractions to Focus on What Matters

If you wear glasses, you’ve likely experienced this: you’re going about your day, thinking everything is fine, only for someone to point out how dirty your lenses are. Or maybe you remove your glasses and suddenly realize how much grime has built up. Once you clean them, it feels like the world has transformed. Colors are brighter, details sharper, and everything is clearer. It’s a moment of revelation.

This experience is a perfect metaphor for starting as a product manager.

When you’re new to product management, your "lenses" often start smudged. You’re navigating uncharted waters, trying to make sense of user needs, stakeholder demands, competitive pressures, and technical constraints—all at the same time. The dirt on your glasses? That’s the noise, distractions, and assumptions clouding your vision. And unless you regularly pause to clean your lenses, you risk making decisions based on an unclear picture.

Identifying the Smudges

Smudges on your glasses aren’t always obvious until you look for them. Similarly, in product management, unclear priorities, undefined goals, or misaligned expectations can obscure your understanding of what’s truly important. These might include:

  • Unvalidated assumptions: Are you building something because you think users want it, or because you know they do?
  • Scope creep: Are new ideas piling on and diluting your focus?
  • Stakeholder noise: Are you prioritizing the loudest voice in the room rather than the actual data?
  • Overwhelming details: Are you so bogged down in specifics that you’re missing the big picture?

The Act of Cleaning Your Glasses

Cleaning your glasses—both literally and metaphorically—requires deliberate effort. Here are some ways to bring clarity to your work as a product manager:

  1. Pause and Reflect: Regularly step back to evaluate what you’re working on and why. Are your efforts aligned with your team’s goals and your users’ needs?
  2. Ask Questions: Don’t shy away from challenging assumptions. If something isn’t clear, dig deeper until it is.
  3. Prioritize Ruthlessly: Not everything can be a priority. Focus on what delivers the most value.
  4. Communicate Clearly: Ensure everyone—from developers to stakeholders—understands the vision, goals, and progress.
  5. Embrace Feedback: Just like someone pointing out your dirty glasses, external perspectives can help you see what you’ve missed.

Seeing the World in HD

Once your lenses are clean, the difference is undeniable. You’re no longer making decisions in a fog. Instead, you see your product roadmap with precision. You’re clearer about what your users need, what your team can deliver, and how you can create meaningful impact.

But here’s the catch: glasses don’t stay clean forever. Smudges will reappear, and the process of cleaning is ongoing. Similarly, maintaining clarity in product management requires constant vigilance. The world—and your product—is always changing. Staying clear demands regular effort.

Final Thoughts

As a product manager, your role is to bring focus and clarity to chaos. To do that, you need to regularly clean your lenses. By acknowledging the smudges, taking steps to clear them away, and committing to an ongoing process of reflection and adjustment, you’ll be better equipped to guide your team, delight your users, and deliver exceptional products.

So the next time you catch yourself squinting through smudged lenses, take a moment to clean them. And while you’re at it, consider what’s clouding your view at work—and how you can clear it away.
