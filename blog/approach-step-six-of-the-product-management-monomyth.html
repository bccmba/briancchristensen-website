Approach - Step Six of the Product Management Monomyth

The prince approaches the sleeping maiden. He then kisses and awakens the sleeping beauty. This kiss breaks the evil fairy spell and both her and her whole kingdom awaken. Although there are several different versions to this story this is the main one most people know.

Last time we spoke about trials, and just like Hercules, the prince in this story had his trials to endure. However, once the prince was able to overcome his trials, he was able to approach the princess and fulfill the quest he had been called on.

Like the prince, we as Product Managers get to have our approach as we make our way through our trials. This is done by one of my favorite ceremonies, sprint demo.

Sprint Demo

The reason sprint demos are my favorite ceremony is that here is where you get to show off your team's work. Here is where you get to stand up in front of your stakeholders and show them "how" you have solved their problems. You get to show the effort of having gone through all of your trials. Finally, you get to make sure you are still on the right approach for your product.

These meetings can be amazing and here are a couple of tips to help them become so:

Get the Stakeholders to the Meeting

I know this one may seem a bit obvious, but sometimes this can be harder than you think. At one company I worked at we had our biweekly sprint demo meeting and most of the time it was just the team presenting to themselves what they had worked on. After a couple of months of this, you could tell no one wanted to be there. It was just another meeting taking up time they could be coding.

So for the next meeting, I personally invited all of the stakeholders to come. I almost had to physically drag one of them there, but they were there. In the end, they all asked if they could attend the next sprint demo because they were amazed at the work we had to show them. We told them we would let them know and resent out the meeting invite. From then on, they attended every sprint demo.

Feedback is Key

The next step after getting the stakeholder there is to get them to give you feedback. Recently one of my teams did a sprint demo to the stakeholders. For the next week, every member of my team told me multiple times how much they had enjoyed the meeting. When I asked why the number one answer they gave me was because of the feedback from the stakeholders.

During the meeting, the stakeholders had asked several questions to each of the developers who had presented. Many of the questions had mostly been clarifying questions, helping the stakeholders understand a bit more on "how" the changes were going to help solve their problem. However, one of the discussions had not gone as smoothly as the rest.

When the developer presented their work, the stakeholder stopped them before they were able to finish their presentation. Although they liked what the developer had done, it really was not what they were expecting. They could not see how what was being shown was going to solve their problem. After talking back and forth, we found an assumption we had made was not correct and we were taking our product in the right direction. Luckily, because this stakeholder was there, we were able to get the clarification we needed and make the changes before we went much further in the wrong direction.

Nobody likes to be told they are wrong, however, in this setting it was great. The developer who raved about how great the meeting went, was the one whose demo went the worst. They had received some great information and were excited to get the product back on track.

Contrast this with a sprint demo where the stakeholder does not say anything to the team when they present them with their work. It is almost worse than if they had not shown up at all.

Once you have overcome the trials of your journey it is great to be able to take the approach to your goal and start to show off all of the great things you have done for your product.
