The Status Quo - Step One of the Product Management Monomyth

June 19, 2021

The Status Quo - Step One of the Product Management Monomyth

It is one of the most recognized shots from Star Wars: A New Hope. Luke Skywalker has just had a fight with his uncle about needing to stay on the farm for one more season. He stands up to leave and his aunt asks him where he is going. Luke's reply is "Looks like I'm going nowhere." Then in the next scene, Luke is scene staring off into the distance as you see the two suns of Tatooine.

At this point, this is where we see Luke Skywalker wrestiling with his surrounds and where he fits into the story. This is the point where the hero is set at the status quo. It has been established, without any other changes, nothing is going to change in Luke's life. He is going to keep working on his uncles farm, he is going to watch all of his friends go off and have their own adventures and everything is going to be the same for him.

The Status Quo

Joseph Campbell sets the starting point of all of the adventures in his monomyth where we see the hero doing the same tasks they have always done. Nothing new is happening in their life and for the unforeseeable future, nothing is going to change the narrative. Campbell refers to this stage as the status quo.

However, it is at this stage, even though the hero is doing the same thing they have always done in the past, there is something there they are longing to do. There is something more they want to be doing if it were not for the status quo preventing them from making this move or transition.

Product Management

This sense of remaining in the status quo can be seen within Product Management as well. Often there are times when the product we are managing feels like it is forever going to be stuck doing the same thing. These feeling can be enhanced if you are currently doing maintance work on your product. You will feel like everyday you are just working on the same things and nothing more is every going to change with your product.

However, much like Luke Skywalker, or any other hero we read about, we too as Product Managers have a longing to do more with our product. We know there is more we could do to make our product work better for the company or for our customers. Unfortunately, many of us will sit in the status quo and will just allow the product to do the same things it has always done. Let me suggest two things you can do to help lift you out of this status quo or at least have the longing to want to move on.

Remember what it was like when you first started

In my career, it has always been amazing at the difference of how Product Managers act when they start. In most cases, I have seen new Product Managers come into an organization and have great plans for making changes. They question the processes and "unwritten" rules. It is through this attitude they are able to help an organization pull themselves out of the status quo ruts and hopefully help them back down the path to be ready to make changes.

The problem lies in what happens over time. I have seen it in my own life where I started at an organization and questioned the process and "unwritten" rules. This has been great and you can see where the organization and your product will see a lift. Then as time goes on, slowly you start to hear yourself say, "that is just the way we always do it around here." Something you would not have said when you first started. Now it just the thing that will drive you further and further into the status quo, with no hope of wanting to leave.

Take stock of where you are with your organiztion and especially with your product and remember back to all of the ideas you had when you first started. The hopefully you can slay the "that is the way we have always done it" monster.

Move from looking behind you to ahead of you

The scene with Luke Skywalker staring off at the setting suns is a great imagary. Although Luke felt like he was stuck where he was at, he was looking forward out into the distance. He could have just head straight to cleaning the R2D2 and C-3PO, but he took the time to stop and look forward.

It is very easy to get into a "build what you are told" pattern with your product. This will cement you further and further into the status quo and make it harder for you to move forward with your product. If you do not take the time to stop and look forward to what your product can do, you are never going to be ready when the opportunity to move to the next stage in the Product Managers journey.
